Participation of Global Powers in the Syrian Civil War

Russia

Russia has clear reasons to try to keep Assad in power, in order to benefit their military. Russia's only military installation in the Mediterranean region is at the Syrian port of Tartus (Ted Galen Carpenter), which would most likely cease to exist if the Assad regime fell. This naval base is very important to Russia in order to remain a global superpower, as without this base, Russia can not fight in the Mediterranean. Russia has had these strategic ties with Syria since the Soviet Union, when Syria was led by Hafez al-Assad, who is the father of Bashar Assad. During the Cold War, Russia supplied Syria with vast amounts of economic and military aid, (Ted Galen Carpenter) and there is no reason to believe that they will not continue to do so.

China

China's motivations to participate are much less strong, as it is purely economic. "China was Syria's largest trading partner in 2011, with Syrian exports to that country totaling more than $2.4 billion. China is also a major participant in Syria's oil industry" (Ted Galen Carpenter) If Assad is removed from power, the new regime will almost definitely not allow China to participate economically in Syria at the same level. The largest force trying to remove Assad is the US, so if Assad is removed from power, then Syria will trade with the US much more than China. However, if Assad remains in power, then Syria will most likely become a much more important economic partner to China, like many other countries, most of which are in Africa.

United States

The United States has no reason to participate in Syria, other than to supposedly "save the people". However, its allies have their own reasons to want to remove Assad. Saudi Arabia wants to remove Assad, because he is Iran's primary ally in the region. (Ted Galen Carpenter) Turkey wants to remove Assad, because the ruling party in Turkey is Sunni, and cannot tolerate seeing Sunni insurgents and civilians being killed anymore. (Ted Galen Carpenter) Israel wants to remove Assad, because Assad has been unfriendly to Israel, having waged several wars since the 1940s. (Ted Galen Carpenter) These are all allies of the US who want to remove Assad, which is a reason that the US is fighting to remove Assad. The other reason is that Assad is allied with Russia, and the US is constantly fighting Russia, so it clearly would like to take power away from Russia. However, the reason that the US gives for its involvement is that it's saving the Syrian people. Secretary of State (at the time) Hillary Clinton accused Russia and China of being "clearly not on the side of the Syrian people." (Ted Galen Carpenter)
